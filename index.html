About Me

I was born and raised in Zimbabwe, and came to the US for college through the United States Achiever's Program. After completing my B.S in Biomedical Engineering in 2018, I took a gap year from my 5-year program and worked that summer with Epicenter - Doctor's Without Boarders, Cameroon, where I conducted a cross-sectional study, assessing the prevalence of Hypertension and Diabetes and associated risk factors in HIV Positive and Negative patients at the Military Hospital of Douala. After my summer work at Epicenter, I worked full-time at Aetion Inc as a Scientific Analyst, where I provided research, analytical and data handling capability on a variety of real-world evidence healthcare case studies, before coming back and completing my M.P.H degree in May 2020. My experiences in analytics and health-tech have inspired me to pursue a career in Quantitative Research and Data Science.
